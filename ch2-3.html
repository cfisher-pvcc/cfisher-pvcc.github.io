Chapter 2: Briefly Define:
  1. Natural Language: The lnaguage we speak and write in our everyday lives.
  2. Pseudocode: Is a notation that means to design and represent algorithms.
  3. Sequential Operation (straight line algorithm): It executes its instructions in a straight line from top to bottom and then stops.
  4. Input:What is put in, taken in, or operated on by any process or system.
  5. Output:A place where power or information leaves a system
  6. Conditional Statement: They are the “question-asking” operations of an algorithm. They allow an algorithm to ask a yes/no question and select the next operation to perform on the basis of the answer to that question. There are a number of ways to phrase a question, but the most common conditional statement is the if/then/else statement.
  7. Boolean Condition: A logical statement that is either TRUE or FALSE
  8. Algorithmic Discovery Process: Finding a solution to a given problem.
Chapter 3: Briefly Describe or Define:
  1. Why Algorithims Should be Correct: Algorithims that are not correct can lead to mistakes that are costly and can be fatal.
  2. Why Algorithims Should be Efficient: An algorithim must b efficient as time and space are not unlimited resources.

  7. Order of Magnitude: an approximation of the logarithm of a value relative to some contextually understood reference value, usually 10, interpreted as the base of the logarithm and the representative of values of magnitude one
  8. Selection Sort: The selection sort “grows” a sorted subsection of the list from the back to the front.
  9. Flop (floating point operation): a measurement of computer performance.
  10. Teraflop: A unit of computing speed equal to one million million (10^12) floating-point operations per second
  11. Petaflop: A unit of computing speed equal to one thousand million million (10^15) floating-point operations per second.
  12. Parallel Processor: A method in computing of running two or more processors (CPUs) to handle separate parts of an overall task.
  13. Data Cleanup Problem:the process of fixing or removing incorrect, corrupted, incorrectly formatted, duplicate, or incomplete data within a dataset.
  14. Binary Search Algorithm: A searching algorithm used in a sorted array by repeatedly dividing the search interval in half.
  15. Pattern Matching: The process of checking a given sequence of tokens or data against a pattern and checking its presence.
Karl Friedrich Gauss:
  1. Give a brief overview of Karl Frederich Gauss' mathematics abilities and scientific achievements: Gauss was the first mathematician to construct a 17-sided heptadecagon using a compass and a straight edge, and more importantly was the first to prove the laws of quadratic reciprocity. He is responsible for the prime number theorem. Gauss's work was instrumental to the understanding of algebra, as he proved its central theorem which states that "every non-constant single-variable polynomial with complex coefficients has at least one complex root." One of Gauss's most important contributions to astronomy stemmed from using conic equations to track the dwarf planet Ceres, whose own discoverer Giuseppe Piazzi could not locate it months after its discovery due to the limitations of available tools.
